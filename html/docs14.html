Difference Between Phrasal Verbs and Idioms

  • Phrasal Verb: A verb combined with a preposition or adverb that changes the verb's meaning.
    Examples: give up (stop trying), turn on (activate)
  • Idiom: A phrase whose meaning is not literal, but culturally understood.
    Examples: kick the bucket (to die), spill the beans (reveal a secret)

Key point: Phrasal verbs are usually verb + preposition/adverb, while idioms often have figurative meanings that don't come from the individual words.
