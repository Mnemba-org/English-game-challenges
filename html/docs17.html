📘 Quick Class

Word Categories: Noun, Verb, Adjective, Adverb

📌 Noun

A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

Examples: teacher, school, car, love
  • She is a doctor.
  • I live in a big city.

📌 Verb

A verb shows an action or state of being.

Examples: run, eat, play, is, jump
  • They run every morning.
  • She is happy today.

📌 Adjective

An adjective describes a noun. It tells more about the noun.

Examples: beautiful, small, red, fast
  • The red apple is sweet.
  • He is a smart boy.

📌 Adverb

An adverb describes a verb, adjective, or another adverb. It tells how, when, where, or how often.

Examples: quickly, very, always, here
  • She speaks softly.
  • He always arrives early.

🔍 Noun vs Verb vs Adjective vs Adverb

  • Noun: thing → book, chair, love
  • Verb: action → read, sit, feel
  • Adjective: describes noun → blue, big, honest
  • Adverb: describes verb/adjective → quickly, very, rarely

Tip: Many adverbs end in "-ly", but not all!
