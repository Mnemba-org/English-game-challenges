Study these English words and their meanings. Then click "Start Game" to test yourself.

  • Abandon - to leave something or someone behind
    Example: The sailors had to abandon the ship during the storm.
  • Brief - short in time or length
    Example: She gave a brief explanation before leaving.
  • Confident - feeling sure about yourself
    Example: He felt confident before the job interview.
  • Define - to explain the meaning of a word
    Example: Can you define the word "freedom"?
  • Effort - the physical or mental energy used to do something
    Example: With enough effort, you can achieve anything.
  • Fragile - easily broken or damaged
    Example: Be careful! That vase is very fragile.
  • Grateful - feeling thankful
    Example: I am grateful for your support and kindness.
  • Harsh - unpleasant or rough in tone or conditions
    Example: The winter was harsh and cold.
  • Ignore - to pay no attention
    Example: You should not ignore important warnings.
  • Justice - fairness or lawfulness
    Example: The court ensured justice was served.
  • Keen - very interested or enthusiastic
    Example: She's keen to start her new job.
  • Limit - a point beyond which something does not or may not extend
    Example: You have reached your data limit.
  • Maintain - to keep something in good condition
    Example: It's important to maintain a healthy diet.
  • Neutral - not taking sides
    Example: The judge must remain neutral.
  • Obvious - easily understood or clear
    Example: It's obvious he didn't study for the test.
  • Prevent - to stop something from happening
    Example: Vaccines help prevent diseases.
  • Quit - to stop doing something
    Example: She decided to quit smoking.
  • Reliable - able to be trusted
    Example: He is a reliable friend.
  • Satisfy - to meet needs or expectations
    Example: The meal didn't satisfy my hunger.
  • Threat - a danger or warning of harm
    Example: Climate change is a real threat.
  • Urgent - needing immediate attention
    Example: This is an urgent matter.
  • Value - the importance or worth of something
    Example: We value your feedback.
  • Witness - someone who sees an event
    Example: The witness gave a statement to the police.
  • Yield - to produce or give way
    Example: The farm yields lots of corn each year.
  • Zeal - great energy or enthusiasm
    Example: She approached her work with zeal.
  • Adapt - to change to fit new conditions
    Example: Animals must adapt to survive.
  • Balance - a state where things are equal or steady
    Example: Try to find a balance between work and rest.
  • Calculate - to find an answer using math
    Example: Can you calculate the total cost?
  • Deserve - to be worthy of something
    Example: She deserves an award for her hard work.
  • Entire - the whole or complete thing
    Example: He ate the entire cake!
  • Familiar - well known or easily recognized
    Example: That song sounds familiar.
  • Genuine - real or authentic
    Example: This is genuine leather.
  • Hesitate - to pause before doing something
    Example: Don't hesitate to ask for help.
  • Impress - to make someone admire you
    Example: He wants to impress his boss.
  • Jealous - feeling unhappy about what others have
    Example: She was jealous of her friend's success.
  • Kindness - the quality of being nice or helpful
    Example: Kindness can change someone's day.
  • Lucky - having good fortune
    Example: You're lucky to have such friends.
  • Mistake - something done wrong
    Example: It was just a small mistake.
  • Notice - to become aware of something
    Example: Did you notice the new painting?
  • Offer - to give or propose something
    Example: He offered to help carry my bag.
