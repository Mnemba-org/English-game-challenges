📘 Quick Class: Idioms & Their Meanings

Learn popular English idioms and their meanings. These expressions help you sound more natural in conversations.

  • Break the ice – To initiate conversation in a social setting
  • Spill the beans – To reveal a secret
  • Hit the sack – To go to bed
  • Let the cat out of the bag – To accidentally reveal a secret
  • Under the weather – Feeling sick or unwell
  • Piece of cake – Something very easy
  • Once in a blue moon – Happens very rarely
  • Cost an arm and a leg – Very expensive
  • Hit the nail on the head – Say something exactly right
  • Kick the bucket – To die
  • Bite the bullet – To bravely face a painful situation
  • When pigs fly – Something that will never happen
  • Break a leg – Good luck!
  • Call it a day – Stop working for the day
  • The ball is in your court – It’s your decision now
  • Burn the midnight oil – Work late into the night
  • Pull someone’s leg – To joke or tease someone
  • Back to the drawing board – Start over after failure
  • Speak of the devil – The person you’re talking about just arrived
  • Feeling blue – Feeling sad

Tips:

  • Try to visualize the idiom to remember it better.
  • Use idioms in daily sentences to practice naturally.
  • Don't translate literally—focus on meaning!
